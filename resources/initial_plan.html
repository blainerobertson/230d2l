Initial Planning Document

Due Saturday of Week 04 by 11:30pm, local time

Last week you learned about developing a purpose statement, focusing on the needs of users, persona development and principles of design and had opportunity to examine mobile web sites and collaborate in a team situation to accomplish a task.

This week we added the concept of sketching wireframes to the planning process and we have begun learning how to write HTML.

Overview

Beginning today we will be working on a website to provide weather information for three cities. You are to produce the initial planning document describing the site purpose, target audience, a persona to represent a member of the audience and produce sketches for how the data will be laid out on the screen for three device sizes (small, medium and large screens). You will build a web page, include all of the components mentioned into the document and post it to your web site with a link to the document in the footer of your home page.

Tasks

  1. Develop a site purpose statement based on the activities from last week and submit it as part of the planning page (due Saturday).
  2. Develop an Audience description using the document from last week and submit it as part of the planning page (due Saturday).
  3. Develop an audience Persona using the Persona materials from last week and submit it as part of the planning page (due Saturday).
  4. Produce three sketches for small, medium and large screen devices using the provided device templates (if desired).
  5. Peer Review: When done, have at least one other member of the class review your document and provide feedback.
    • Revise the document (including sketches) as needed, taking the peer review into consideration. You will be accountable for reporting the peer feedback and your response to it.

Submission

  1. Create a web page for all components of this assessment.
  2. Name the file following the web naming rules and store it in your web site.
  3. Include meaningful headings for each of the sections: Purpose Statement, Audience Description, Persona, Sketches (wireframes) and Peer Review.
  4. Add the appropriate content directly beneath the related heading.
  5. Post the web page to your web site.
  6. Place a link to the planning page in your temporary home page.
  7. Test the link from your home page to the planning page in the browser from your web site to insure everything works.

Grading Matrix

This assessment will be graded for three objectives: 1, 4 and 6.

Objective 1 - 10 points

  1. The page is valid HTML5 - 3 points
  2. The page has semantic header, nav, main and footer elements - 4 points
  3. The page has a meaningful title and each section has an appropriate heading - 3 points

Objective 4 - 25 points

All content must be in the planning document.

  1. The purpose statement clearly describes why the weather site is being built - 5 points.
  2. The target audience is described in a meaningful way and all aspects of the visitor consideration document are represented - 5 points
  3. A persona is present to represent one of the focus groups of the audience and all aspects of the persona are present - 5 points
  4. Three sketches for small, medium and large screens are included in the planning document - 10 points

Objective 6 - 5 points

The peer review information is included in the planning document.

  1. The name(s) of the peer reviewer(s) are listed - 2 points
  2. Peer review comments are included in the planning document and what you changed (if anything) as a result of the feedback - 3 points
